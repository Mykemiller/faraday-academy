## 3. Flags — claims corrected, softened, or discrepant

Per the quality rule: **flag rather than soften.** Three items required a flag.

### (a) ⚑ CORRECTED FACTUAL ERROR — Module 4 title in the earlier internal draft
The catalog/draft titled Module 4 **"Frederick, Maryland — The First Major Moratorium Victory."** The public record contradicts this on two counts:
- **Not a "victory"** for opposition in the plain sense — the residents' referendum was **kept off the ballot** by the courts (S6, S7), and the county proceeded with its overlay zone.
- **No sourced basis for "The First"** — no source establishes Frederick as the first major moratorium anywhere.

**Action taken:** Module 4 was retitled *"Anatomy of a Contested Approval"* and rewritten to the verified record, with the inaccurate original called out inside the module for transparency. **This is the single clearest example of the pipeline catching a fabrication-shaped claim before publish.**

### (b) ⚑ INTERNAL-RECORD DISCREPANCY — "Resolved" status
Faraday's internal **Community Resistance Monitor** (S19) logs Frederick MD, King George VA, and Boardman OR as **"✅ Resolved (Q1 2026)."** The public record shows this is **premature/incorrect** for at least Frederick and King George: Frederick litigation and new restrictions ran into **July 2026** (S6, S7); the King George/Amazon zoning dispute was **still heading to the Virginia Supreme Court** (S8, and Appendix B). **Action taken:** the script never repeats "Resolved"; Module 4 explicitly flags the discrepancy and teaches "check the primary record before calling a market settled." **Recommended follow-up:** correct the internal Monitor's status field (owner: Community Relations / Curriculum agent).

### (c) ⚠️ INTERNAL-ONLY CLAIM — softened "statistically correlates"
The internal Tower 11 page (S19) asserts the Good Neighbor Playbook *"statistically correlates with approval success."* We could **not locate a published statistic** behind "statistically." **Action taken:** the script was **softened** to *"broadly associated with approval success"* with an explicit parenthetical that no study quantifying the effect is known to us — kept as directional, not measured. **Recommended:** cite a specific study if the Community Relations agent has one, or leave as directional.

## Appendix A — Provenance note on the Data Center Watch figures
The Q1-2026 figures (Claims 1, 5, 6, 11) originate with **Data Center Watch** (an opposition-tracking project). Its own report page (https://www.datacenterwatch.org/report — initial headline: **$64B** blocked/delayed) blocked automated retrieval (HTTP 403). The numbers used in the script are therefore cited to **NBC News (S3)**, which reports the tracker's Q1-2026 dataset directly, and are **independently corroborated** by *Fortune* (2026-06-16, https://fortune.com/2026/06/16/data-center-opposition-construction-delays-blocks-report/) and *Tom's Hardware*. Treat as reputable-secondary, not primary; a primary-source confirmation of the exact figures is a sensible pre-publish step.
